How Our Brain Is Controlling Us

On the subconscious, dopamine loops, and reclaiming agency.

Dec 19, 2024

We like to believe we are the pilots of our own lives. But the neuroscience suggests otherwise. We are merely passengers driven by a silent autopilot: the subconscious mind.

The College Illusion

College life is a battleground. It is an environment designed to exploit the brain’s reward circuitry. When you reach for your phone the moment you wake up, that isn't you deciding. It’s a dopamine loop cemented by repetition.

Rewiring the System

Awareness is the first step. You must observe the urge without acting on it. Identity is the second step. Stop saying "I'm trying to focus" and start saying "I am a person who focuses."

We build systems to scale code and trade. It is time we build systems to scale our own minds.

Why NIT & IIT Exposure Is Different

It's not about the books. It's about who is reading them next to you.

Jan 10, 2025

The syllabus is available online. The books are the same. So, what actually creates the gap?

The "Filter" of Excellence

Every single person here has crossed a massive barrier (JEE Mains/Advanced) just to enter the gate. You are surrounded by the top 1% of the country's intellect. This isn't just a crowd; it is a curated network of high-performers from every state in India.

Diverse, On-Ground Exposure

When you live with people from diverse cultures who are all competitively driven, your worldview expands. You see how a person from the South approaches logic differently than someone from the North. You compete, you collaborate, and you realize that "smart" has many definitions.

This environment forces you to level up. You don't work hard to stand out; you work hard just to fit in. The true value of these institutes is the peer group that destroys your mediocrity.
